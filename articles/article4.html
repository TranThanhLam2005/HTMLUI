Da Nang Police dismantle $40 million football betting ring during Euro 2024

22/06/2024 12:33 (GMT+07:00)

This operation is a major victory for the Vietnamese police in their fight against illegal online gambling, a crime that has become more sophisticated with the use of high-tech platforms. The case demonstrates the reach of betting networks and highlights the ongoing efforts to curb such activities.

Arrest of Three Football Betting Ringleaders

On the evening of June 21, Hai Chau District Police in Da Nang, in collaboration with specialized forces, arrested three key suspects involved in a massive football betting ring with transactions exceeding VND 1,000 billion (nearly $40 million). The suspects, identified as Nguyen Huynh Van Doi (born in 1966), Tran Dinh Ba (born in 1958), and Doan Trong (born in 1965), were apprehended for organizing and leading the illegal betting operation. The three men resided in Da Nang, with Van Doi and Ba living in Hai Chau District, while Trong resided in Son Tra District.

Discovery Through Cyber Surveillance

The arrests followed months of cyber surveillance by Hai Chau District Police and the Department of Cyber Security and High-Tech Crime Prevention. Authorities identified a group of individuals running and participating in illegal football betting on websites like www.viva6889.com and www.sporty88.com. The police's use of advanced technology to monitor online activities allowed them to track the suspects' movements and gather significant evidence against the betting ring.

Confession and Scope of the Operation

The suspects confessed that since August 2023, they had been operating a betting network with an initial limit of 3 million USD (over VND 60 billion) per month, granted by an overseas bookmaker. This amount was distributed to lower-level agents who managed the network further. The operation generated approximately 8 million USD in revenue each month, resulting in a total transaction value exceeding VND 1,000 billion ($40 million) across various accounts linked to the websites.

Seizure of Evidence and Ongoing Investigation

During the raid, police seized more than 15,000 pages of documents that recorded betting transactions exceeding VND 1,000 billion. In addition, they confiscated multiple savings books and froze billions of VND in the suspects' bank accounts. Following the arrests, Hai Chau District Police and the Department of Cyber Security dispatched a team to Ho Chi Minh City to arrest two more individuals who had supplied the betting network. The case is now under further investigation, with authorities seeking to dismantle the entire operation and identify all parties involved.
